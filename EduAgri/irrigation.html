Irrigation Techniques

Learn modern and traditional irrigation methods to improve crop yield and conserve water.

Introduction

Irrigation techniques are methods of supplying water to crops when natural rainfall is insufficient. Effective irrigation improves crop yield, conserves water, and reduces the risk of crop failure. Farming has evolved from traditional flood irrigation to modern approaches like sprinklers, drip systems, and sensor-based automation. These techniques allow precise water delivery, ensuring sustainability and efficiency in agriculture.

Topics Covered

  • Surface irrigation: furrow, basin, and border methods for traditional water management.
  • Sprinkler systems: overhead watering that simulates rainfall efficiently.
  • Drip / micro-irrigation: delivers water directly to the plant roots, reducing wastage.
  • Water scheduling & efficiency: planning irrigation cycles for optimal crop growth.
  • Automation & sensor-based irrigation: modern smart systems that detect soil moisture and deliver water automatically.
